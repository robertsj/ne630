Lesson 7

Learning Objectives

Students will be able to…

Macro Objectives

  • Estimate the number of elastic collisions a neutron of energy \(E\) must make with a nucleus of mass number to slow to an energy \(E'\).

Micro Objectives

  • For a given target nuclide, predict whether elastic or inelastic scattering dominates the slowing of neutrons.
  • Compute the minimum, maximum, and expected outgoing energy of a neutron of energy after it scatters elastically (and isotropically) off of a given target nucleus.
  • Compute the probability that a neutron of energy \(E\) loses a given fraction of its energy following an elastic collision.
  • Derive the slowing down decrement \(\xi\) for a material with multiple nuclides.

Key Terms

  • slowing down decrement
  • elastic scattering
  • inelastic scattering
  • isotropic
  • center of mass (system)
  • laboratory (system)
