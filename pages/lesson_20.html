Lesson 20

Learning Objectives

Macro Objectives

Students will be able to quantify the impact of changes in the fuel temperature on the reactivity.

Micro Objectives

  • Explain the relationship between reactivity and the multiplication factor.
  • Explain why the fuel temperature coefficient (FTC) is usually strongly negative.
  • Explain how the fuel temperature coefficient of a sodium-cooled fast reactor differs from that of a light-water reactor.

Key Terms

  • reactivity
  • units of reactivity: dollars and cents; \(\Delta k/k\); percent-milli (pcm)
  • fuel temperature coefficient (FTC)
