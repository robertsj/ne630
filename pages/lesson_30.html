Lesson 30

Learning Objectives

Macro Objectives

Students will be able to show that neutrons are conserved (or not) in a spatial volume given the neutron flux and appropriate cross-section data.

Micro Objectives

  • Write down, in plain English, a statement that describes neutron balance in a control volume.
  • Explain what the neutron current \(\vec{J}\) represents. Note that a vector can also be written \(\mathbf{x}\).
  • Given a neutron flux \(\phi(\vec{r})\) and a diffusion coefficient , compute the neutron current.
  • Integrate the neutron continuity equation (Lewis, Eq. (6.10)) or the neutron diffusion equation (Lewis, Eq. (6.12)) over a control volume.
  • Describe how the neutron diffusion equation is similar to and different from other conservation equations in engineering.

Key Terms

  • leakage
  • diffusion coefficient
  • Fick’s law
  • gradient (of a function), \(\vec{\nabla} f\)
  • divergence (of a vector), \(\vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{v}\)
  • Laplacian (of a function), \(\vec{\nabla} f = \vec{\Delta} f\)

Homework

  • [30.1] Consider a cubic, homogeneous system defined for \(x \in [-1, 1]\), \(y \in [-1, 1]\), and \(z \in [-1, 1]\) characterized by a diffusion coefficient and absorption cross section . For some (unknown) neutron source distribution \(s(\vec{r})\), suppose the steady-state neutron flux is \(\phi(\vec{r}) = \phi(x, y, z) = (1-x^2)(1-y^2)\). Compute the (1) total absorption rate in the system and (2) the leakage through each of its 6 faces. Assuming that the medium is nonmultiplying, evaluate \(\int^1_{-1}\int^1_{-1}\int^1_{-1} s(x,y,z)dxdydz\).
